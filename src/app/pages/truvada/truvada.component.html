Truvada (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir) Information

What is Truvada?

Truvada is a prescription medication combining emtricitabine and tenofovir disoproxil fumarate. It is used for HIV treatment and for pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) to reduce the risk of HIV infection in at-risk individuals.

How It Works

Truvada prevents HIV from multiplying by inhibiting reverse transcriptase, an enzyme HIV needs to reproduce. When used as PrEP, it helps protect HIV-negative individuals from infection.

Common Uses

  • HIV treatment (in combination with other antiretrovirals)
  • Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) for HIV prevention

Dosage

Truvada is typically taken as one tablet once daily, with or without food. Always follow your healthcare provider’s instructions for treatment or PrEP.

Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Headache
  • Fatigue
  • Kidney function changes (rare)

Warnings

Do not start or stop Truvada without consulting a healthcare provider. Regular monitoring of kidney function is recommended during use. Not a cure for HIV — safe practices are still required.
