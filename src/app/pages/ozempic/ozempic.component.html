Ozempic (Semaglutide) Information

What is Ozempic?

Ozempic is a prescription medication containing semaglutide, used to improve blood sugar control in adults with type 2 diabetes. It is also widely used to support weight loss under medical supervision.

How It Works

Ozempic is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that helps regulate blood sugar by increasing insulin production, slowing digestion, and reducing appetite, leading to improved glucose control and weight reduction.

Common Uses

  • Type 2 Diabetes Management
  • Weight Loss (off-label or prescribed programs)
  • Reducing cardiovascular risk in certain patients

Dosage

Ozempic is typically injected once weekly. Common starting dose is 0.25 mg, increasing to 0.5 mg or 1 mg depending on response and medical guidance.

Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Decreased appetite
  • Constipation

Warnings

Ozempic may not be suitable for individuals with a history of thyroid cancer or certain endocrine conditions. Always consult a healthcare provider before starting treatment.
